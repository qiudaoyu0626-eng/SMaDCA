# Methodology

## Core calculations

- Monthly surplus = after-tax income − essential expenses − flexible budget − required debt payments.
- Savings rate = monthly surplus ÷ after-tax income.
- Emergency coverage = liquid emergency cash ÷ monthly essential expenses.
- Asset weight = asset value ÷ total long-term portfolio value.
- Drift in percentage points = current weight − target weight.
- Contribution-led rebalance: direct new money to the most underweight eligible sleeves first.
- Future value of monthly contributions: `P × (((1 + r/12)^(12y) − 1) / (r/12))`; label all results nominal scenarios.

## Risk classification

- Defensive: equity below 30%.
- Balanced: equity 30–60%.
- Growth: equity 60–80%.
- Aggressive: equity 80–95% with meaningful diversification.
- Concentrated: any narrow country, sector, theme, or single security dominates the result, regardless of total equity weight.

These are communication labels, not regulatory classifications.

## Portfolio checks

1. Liquidity: near-term goals and emergency reserves must not depend on market prices.
2. Concentration: flag narrow sleeves above 30%; treat 40% or more as requiring explicit conviction and drawdown acceptance.
3. Overlap: inspect whether global, US, technology, and Nasdaq funds hold many of the same companies.
4. Home and currency risk: balance domestic living liabilities against foreign-asset and foreign-currency exposure.
5. Defensive ballast: evaluate whether cash, high-quality bonds, or gold are present for a stated purpose.
6. Complexity: every additional fund must add a distinct exposure or implementation advantage.
7. Costs: compare total expense ratio, tracking difference, subscription/redemption costs, taxes, and platform restrictions when available.

## Decision hierarchy

Apply in order:

1. Stop high-cost debt compounding against the user.
2. Protect essential liquidity.
3. Set a sustainable contribution rate.
4. Choose a diversified target allocation matching the horizon and drawdown tolerance.
5. Select low-cost implementation products.
6. Automate and rebalance.
7. Consider tactical tilts only within a capped satellite allocation.
